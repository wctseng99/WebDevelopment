The Best Series According to Jim

My top 3 series of all-time.

Breaking Bad

Breaking Bad" is a critically acclaimed American television series that first aired from 2008 to 2013. Created by Vince Gilligan, the show follows the transformation of a high school chemistry teacher, Walter White, into a ruthless and dangerous methamphetamine manufacturer.

The series is set in Albuquerque, New Mexico, and explores the consequences of Walter's decision to enter the illegal drug trade. Walter, played by Bryan Cranston, initially turns to manufacturing methamphetamine to secure his family's financial future after being diagnosed with terminal lung cancer. He teams up with Jesse Pinkman, a former student and small-time methamphetamine producer, played by Aaron Paul.

As the story unfolds, Walter's descent into the criminal underworld gradually transforms him into the notorious drug lord known as "Heisenberg." He becomes more ruthless and willing to do whatever it takes to protect his empire and provide for his family. Throughout the series, Walter's actions have far-reaching consequences, affecting not only his own life but also those around him.

Prison Break

"Prison Break" is an American television series that originally aired from 2005 to 2009, with a revival season in 2017. Created by Paul Scheuring, the show centers around a thrilling escape plan orchestrated by a structural engineer, Michael Scofield, to break his wrongly convicted brother, Lincoln Burrows, out of prison.

The story begins with Lincoln Burrows, played by Dominic Purcell, being sentenced to death for a crime he claims he didn't commit a conspiracy to assassinate the brother of the Vice President of the United States. Convinced of his brother's innocence, Michael Scofield, portrayed by Wentworth Miller, devises an elaborate scheme to free Lincoln from Fox River State Penitentiary, where he is incarcerated.

Michael deliberately gets himself imprisoned in the same facility, using his extensive knowledge of the prison's design and infrastructure to plan an escape. He tattoos the entire prison's layout on his body, assembling a team of fellow inmates to assist him in the escape. Each member of the team possesses unique skills and plays a crucial role in executing the intricate plan.

As the series progresses, the escape becomes just the beginning of an elaborate conspiracy involving a clandestine organization known as "The Company." Michael and his allies find themselves entangled in a web of intrigue, facing dangerous enemies and constantly being pursued. They must navigate various prisons, evade capture, and uncover the truth behind the conspiracy while trying to stay alive.

Attack on Titan

"Attack on Titan" is a Japanese manga series created by Hajime Isayama, which was later adapted into an anime television series. The story takes place in a world where humanity is on the brink of extinction, as giant humanoid creatures known as Titans roam the land, devouring humans without remorse.

The narrative centers around Eren Yeager, the main protagonist, who lives within the walls of a massive city that serves as humanity's last stronghold. These walls were built to protect humans from the Titans, who have relentlessly hunted and terrorized humanity for over a century. Eren, along with his friends Mikasa Ackerman and Armin Arlert, dreams of exploring the world beyond the walls and eradicating the Titans.

However, their lives are forever changed when a colossal Titan breaches the outer wall, allowing the smaller Titans to invade the city. In the chaos, Eren witnesses the destruction and loss firsthand, fueling his determination to become a member of the military and join the fight against the Titans. He enlists in the Survey Corps, an elite group tasked with venturing outside the walls to confront the Titans directly.

As the series progresses, Eren and his companions uncover dark secrets about the Titans, their origins, and the history of humanity itself. The story delves into themes of survival, identity, political intrigue, and the nature of power. The characters face moral dilemmas, internal conflicts, and the harsh realities of a world threatened by both external and internal forces.
